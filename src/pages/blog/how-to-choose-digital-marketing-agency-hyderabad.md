Hyderabad has hundreds of agencies calling themselves "digital marketing experts." Most sell the same package to every business. Here's how to find one that actually moves your numbers.

## Start with outcomes, not services

Any agency can list "SEO, social media, ads." That tells you nothing. Ask instead:

- **What result will this produce, and how will we measure it?**
- **Which metric ties to revenue** — enquiries, bookings, sales — not likes and impressions?
- **Can you show a real project** with before/after numbers?

If the answers are vague or all about "brand awareness," walk away. Marketing that can't be measured usually isn't working.

## Questions to ask before you sign

1. **Who actually does the work?** Many agencies outsource. Meet the people on your account.
2. **What does the first 90 days look like?** A serious agency has a clear process, not just "we'll post content."
3. **Do you understand the Hyderabad market?** Local search behaviour, languages, and competition matter for local businesses.
4. **What happens if it doesn't work?** Look for accountability, not lock-in contracts.

## Red flags to avoid

- Guarantees of "#1 on Google in 30 days." Nobody can promise that — Google doesn't work that way.
- Reports full of vanity metrics (followers, impressions) with no link to sales.
- No portfolio, or a portfolio of templates that all look the same.
- Pressure to sign a long contract before any results.

## What good looks like

The right agency treats marketing as a **system**: your brand, website, ads, content, and automation all pulling in the same direction, measured against real business outcomes. They should be able to explain exactly how each rupee of spend is meant to come back as revenue.
